# Waimakariri District Council — RFI Rules & Practice

## 1. Statutory framework

- Operates under the **Building Act 2004**. Statutory processing window: **20 working days** (10 working days for MultiProof) [1][2].
- "If your plans are not compliant with the Building Code or further detail is required, you will be sent a Request for Further Information (RFI). The 20 day processing period is then suspended until the requested information is received." [2]
- "If any further information is needed while your consent is reviewed, the processing timeframe is put on hold then continued once all of the information has been received." [1]

## 2. Published RFI / pre-application guidance

The canonical published guide is **"Let's Get it Right — Building Consents" v3, December 2023** (QD-BU-Guide-001) [2]. It walks through 17 numbered steps from "Do you need a building consent?" through Code Compliance Certificate audit.

WDC also publishes a series of **Building Fact Sheets** [3] — at least 8 in the first index page (pagination indicates ≥3 pages, more not yet retrieved):
- Access Around House Building Sites
- Amendments and Minor Variations to Building Consents (Jan 2024)
- Building Change of Use
- Building Inspections (Sep 2025)
- Building Location Certificate
- Building on a Rural Site
- Certificates of Acceptance (2026 update)
- Code Compliance Certificate

WDC does **not** publish a dedicated "Avoiding RFIs" page comparable to CCC's. Guidance is embedded in the master guide and the topic fact sheets.

## 3. Vetting & acceptance process

WDC has an explicit **two-stage post-RFI process** that is unusual and important:

- **Step 4 — Lodgement vetting**: "Applications will be vetted by Council staff to ensure completeness." Incomplete/incorrect applications are rejected: "Application forms with incomplete information or that are incorrectly filled in will cause your application to be rejected and you will then have to re-apply with complete/correct information." [2]
- **Step 5 — Application Assessment** (BCO): RFI issued here if non-compliant or insufficient detail. "The 20 day processing period is then suspended until the requested information is received." [2]
- **Step 6 — Application Validation** (Senior Building Officer): a **second QA gate** after assessment. "When the Building Officer is satisfied your application is fully compliant with the Building Code... This is a quality assurance check used to certify that the relevant documentation accurately represents how compliance will be achieved." [2]
  - "Please note: Not all jobs require validation, this will be determined at the time the consent is allocated to a Building Consent Officer." [2]
  - WDC's web page explicitly notes validation **can produce further RFIs**: consent "may be sent for a final quality assurance check, known as validation, and depending on the findings, **further information may be requested**." [1]

> **Key signal for ConsentIQ**: WDC RFIs come from two distinct sources — BCO (Step 5) and Senior BO Validation (Step 6). Categorise these separately; they may have different remediation patterns.

## 4. Common RFI categories

The "Let's Get it Right" guide does not enumerate common RFI categories explicitly. Implicit categories from Steps 3–5 [2]:

- **Form completeness** — incomplete/incorrect Form 2 → rejection (not RFI).
- **Building Code compliance pathway** — applicant must show how compliance is achieved via one of:
  - Acceptable Solution / Verification Method
  - Determination
  - Current national multi-use approval (MultiProof)
  - Current product certificate (CodeMark)
  - Alternative Solution
- **Producer Statements** — "A Producer Statement may be required for certain work within the project (e.g. in the design or after construction)." Used as supporting evidence; signed by registered engineer / design pro / builder. BCA decides whether to rely on it.
- **Compliance Schedule** — required for specified systems (typically commercial); performance standards for inspection / maintenance / reporting must be supplied.
- **Restricted Building Work (RBW)** — LBP designer required; Certificate of Design Work; record of work for subsequent LBPs.
- **Resource consent / s37 notice** — where District/Regional Plan compliance is materially affected, BCA must issue s37 notice; may halt or stage work until RC granted.
- **Geotechnical** — "Geotechnical Report" defined in glossary; required where soil stability/foundations need engineering recommendations.

## 5. Canterbury-specific overlays (TC zones, foundations, flood)

- **WDC has significant TC2 and TC3 land** — Kaiapoi, Pines Beach, Kairaki and surrounding areas were heavily affected by the 2010–2011 Canterbury earthquakes; portions are former residential red zone, others are TC3 [4][5].
- **TC3 foundation rule (MBIE)** — site-specific geotech investigation + specific engineering foundation design; foundations to tolerate ≥300 mm global lateral displacement [4][5].
- **PIM s37 hazard flagging** — WDC will flag flooding, erosion, subsidence, slippage in PIM [2].
- **District/Regional Plan dependencies** — WDC explicitly may issue s37 notices halting or staging work [2].

## 6. Processing KPIs / published stats

*Not found in public sources.* The guide states statutory limits but does not publish RFI rate, hold time, or acceptance failure rate.

## 7. RFI delivery mechanism

- Inspection booking via phone (03 311 8906) or email (`bcbooking@wmk.govt.nz`); building enquiries `buildinginfo@wmk.govt.nz`; general office `office@wmk.govt.nz`; main line 0800 965 468 [2][6].
- Online lodgement via Council portal (registration required); electronic submissions must be PDF [6].
- *Not stated explicitly: which BCA software WDC uses to deliver RFIs (Objective Build / AlphaOne / GoGet / etc.) and the email pattern for RFI replies.* The guide does not name the system.

## 8. Fees / re-vetting

- "Whether a deposit or the full fee is required to be paid at the time of lodgement will be determined by the Schedule of Fees." [2]
- Fees due in full before consent issued [2].
- 12-month lapse rule: consent lapses if work not started within 12 months unless work-start extension paid [2].
- Development Contributions, additional inspection fees, and amendment processing fees due before CCC issued [2].
- *Specific RFI / re-vetting fees: not in the guide; need to pull WDC fees schedule directly.*

## 9. Open questions / gaps in public info
