# Christchurch City Council — RFI Rules & Practice

## 1. Statutory framework

- Operates under the **Building Act 2004**. Statutory processing window is **20 working days** (10 working days for MultiProof / BuiltReady) [1].
- Day 1 is the next working day after receipt [1].
- "During the processing of your application, the **statutory clock stops** if we have to send you a request for information (RFI)." Once info is provided, "the statutory clock will be started again and processing of the application will resume." [1]

## 2. Published RFI / pre-application guidance

CCC publishes a dedicated **"Avoiding requests for information (RFIs)"** page — the single most important source for what triggers RFIs at CCC [2]. Categories listed verbatim below in §4.

CCC also publishes formal **check sheets** as downloadable forms [3]:

| Form | Title | Last updated |
|---|---|---|
| **B-061** | Check sheet — For Project Information Memorandum (PIM) | 5 May 2023, v8 |
| **B-062** | Check sheet — For Single Residential Dwelling and Accessory Building Application | 16 Dec 2025, v22 |
| **B-063** | Check sheet — For Commercial Building Consent | 16 Dec 2025, v24 |

CCC explicitly says: *"The B-062 application checksheet is a handy checklist for ensuring all items have been considered when lodging an application for building consent. We recommend you submit one with every application."* [2]

Other topic-specific check sheets [3]:
- B-052 — Backflow Prevention Device
- B-054 — Residential or small heated pool
- B-065 — Access and Facilities for Persons with Disabilities (template)
- B-068 — Specified Systems Guidance
- B-308 — Solid fuel burning appliance (residential)

## 3. Vetting & acceptance process

- **Pre-acceptance / vetting** is distinct from technical processing. CCC describes it as a 48–96 hour completeness check; **incomplete applications are rejected outright with a non-acceptance letter, not RFI'd** [1].
- RFIs are issued only **during the technical assessment phase, after acceptance** [1].
- "All applications will incur a fee for the time spent assessing the application regardless of whether they are accepted or not." [1]

## 4. Common RFI categories (verbatim from CCC's "Avoiding RFIs") [2]

### Residential

- **Weather tightness** — Plans/specs do not demonstrate compliance with E2; alternative solutions to E2 not identified.
- **Site and floor levels** — Levels in flood-prone areas not specified in **Christchurch Drainage Datum**. CCC publishes a floor-level map for flood-prone areas (Flood Management Areas).
- **Structural & geotechnical engineering**:
  - "The foundation design is not coordinated with the recommendations from the geotechnical investigation report." (Geotech engineer must confirm in writing.)
  - "No confirmation of whether a producer statement for construction monitoring (PS4) will be supplied" — must include schedule of construction monitoring + PS4 confirmation.
  - "Supporting documentation is not provided where barriers to upper floors or balconies require specific engineering design (SED)." Provide PS1.
- **Quality of application** — "Essential items are missing." Fix: submit B-062 with every application.
- **Planning issues not identified early** — Get a PIM and/or pre-application meeting.
- **Products information** — Lack of supporting docs for proprietary products. Provide test results / BRANZ appraisals; for CodeMark, provide certificate of conformity + how it meets conditions/limitations.

### Commercial

- **Intended use of building not clearly defined** — must state per Building Code A1 classified uses.
- **Staging not pre-agreed** — engage CCC early; consider fit-outs.
- **Weather tightness (E2)** — alt solutions not identified; design features report often appropriate.
- **Access and accessible routes**:
  - Accessible route from boundary/car park into building insufficiently detailed (D1).
  - "The Council generally slopes the footpath at 2% (1:50) and does not normally permit re-grading."
  - Wheelchair car park placement (no travelling behind parked vehicles).
  - Isolated steps on access routes — D1.3.3(i) prohibits.
- **Design coordination** — architectural not coordinated with structural / fire / civil / HVAC.
- **Insulation of building envelope (H1)** — From Nov 2022, higher insulation requirements for new buildings >300 m²; modelling method requires qualified consultant report.
- **H1.3.5 artificial lighting energy efficiency** — required for commercial / communal non-residential >300 m². Include statement from lighting designer (e.g. NZS 4243.2:2007 schedule method) + calculations; or include H1.3.5 in electrical engineer PS1.
- **Sanitary facilities (G1/AS1)** — no justification for proposed numbers; use MBIE toilet calculator.
- **Fire engineering**:
  - No confirmation of fire engineer construction monitoring.
  - C/VM2 documentation incomplete (per FEB).
  - Emergency lighting design lacks PS1 + PS4 confirmation.
- **Structural & geotech (commercial)** — same foundation/geotech coordination + PS4 issues; secondary/ancillary SED elements (barriers, seismic bracing, suspended ceilings, plant platforms) lack PS1.
- **Other authorisations missing**:
  - Network utility approvals (water/wastewater/stormwater capacity) — specific CCC email addresses for each.
  - Other Council units: structures over footpath (asset.protection@), grease management non-underground (tradewaste@).
- **Mechanical ventilation** — drawings/specs/calcs not included where required (G4); G1/AS1 natural ventilation cannot be applied to spaces generating G4.3.3 contaminants.

## 5. Canterbury-specific overlays

- **Christchurch Drainage Datum** — required for site/floor levels in flood-prone areas; CCC publishes a floor-level map [2].
- **TC zones (post-Canterbury earthquake)** — flat residential green-zone land is divided into TC1 / TC2 / TC3 based on liquefaction susceptibility. TC2 typically uses standard shallow geotech; **TC3 requires site-specific geotechnical investigation and specific engineering foundation design**, and foundations must tolerate ≥300 mm global lateral displacement [4][5].
- **Foundation/geotech coordination** is the most heavily emphasised RFI trigger at CCC — geotech engineer must confirm in writing that foundation design complies with their recommendations [2].

## 6. Processing KPIs / published stats

- Statutory limit 20 working days; reduced to 10 for MultiProof/BuiltReady [1].
- No granular RFI-rate or hold-time statistics found on CCC's official pages. Independent reporting cites avg ~19 days for applicants to respond to RFIs and ~34 days total — but that source is non-official and is excluded.
- *Not found in public CCC sources: % of consents that get RFIs, average time on hold.*

## 7. RFI delivery mechanism

- Applications via **My Consents portal** (online), post (PO Box 73013, Christchurch 8154), or in-person (53 Hereford Street) [6].
- *Not stated explicitly on the official pages reviewed: the delivery channel for RFIs themselves (portal vs email).* CCC uses Objective Build / similar BCA workflow software but this is not documented on the public pages reviewed.

## 8. Fees / re-vetting

- "All applications will incur a fee for the time spent assessing the application regardless of whether they are accepted or not." [1]
- Fee schedule: published under Long-Term Plan / Annual Plan fees and charges (2025/2026) [3]; specific RFI / re-vetting fee amounts not extracted in this pass.

## 9. Open questions / gaps in public info

- RFI delivery channel (portal vs email) not stated on processing page.
- No published % of consents receiving RFIs or average hold time.
- No specific re-vetting fee documented on the forms-guides-fees landing page; need to drill into the fees-and-charges PDF.
- B-062 / B-063 check sheet contents not yet pulled — these are the closest CCC has to a "common RFI" enumeration and should be downloaded for the taxonomy build.
